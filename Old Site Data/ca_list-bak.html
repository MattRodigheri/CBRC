California Bird List of the

Western Field Ornithologists’
California Bird Records Committee

Updated 11 February 2009

The sequence of birds on this list has been updated to follow the 7th edition of the A.O.U Check-List of North American Birds, and supplements.

A total of 641 species is included on this list of California birds, ten of which are non-native species, and two of which have been extirpated within historical times. An additional six species have been recorded in the state, but are of uncertain natural occurrence and therefore are included in a supplemental list following the main list. To be considered accepted on the California list, at least one record of the species must be reviewed and accepted by the California Bird Records Committee (CBRC). Species on this list lacking annotation (or marked with just an E or I) are supported by at least one extant specimen for which there is convincing evidence that it was obtained in California. Species marked with a non-specimen annotation are supported by at least one record for which there is one or more photographs (P), videotapes (V), or tape recordings of vocalization (T) that clearly demonstrate definitive characters and for which there is convincing evidence that the photographs, video, or recording was made in California. Species marked with an S are only supported by sight records with one or more convincing written descriptions. Documentation supporting all records reviewed by the CBRC (except for the physical specimens in specimen-supported records) is permanently archived at the Western Foundation of Vertebrate Zoology, 439 Calle San Pablo, Camarillo, California 93012-8506, and is available to researchers.

The CBRC solicits information on all occurrences in California of species on its Review List (those annotated below with an asterisk), as well as species unrecorded in California. In general, review species average four or fewer occurrences per year in California, and have been recorded fewer than 100 times. Documentation should be sent to Guy McCaskie, California Bird Records Committee, P.O. Box 275, Imperial Beach, CA 91933-0275 (or by e-mail to guymcc@pacbell.net). Documentation concerning the potential establishment of exotic species not currently on the state list is also sought.

Supplemental List
This list includes those species recorded in California for which the CBRC has determined that all records are of uncertain natural occurrence. Thus, the CBRC could not reach a consensus judgement, given available evidence, as to whether or not records of these species involved true vagrants or escapes from captivity. Species with questionable natural occurrence (those judged by the CBRC to be escapees) are not admitted to the Supplemental List.
